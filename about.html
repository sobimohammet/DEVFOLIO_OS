A demanding schedule, limited resources, and repeated restarts tested that decision more than once. But every time I stepped away, I came back. Values & Philosophy

"I didn't wait for the perfect conditions. I built with what I had, learned from what broke, and kept going when it made no sense to. That's not a strategy — that's the only way forward I know."
— Sobi Mohamed
01
CRAFT > CARGO-CULT

I'd rather deeply understand one tool than superficially use ten. Every abstraction I reach for, I've tried to understand what's underneath it first. Real mastery comes from first principles, not from copy-pasting Stack Overflow.

02
SHIP > PERFECT

Software that exists and works beats software that's ideal and hypothetical. I maintain a strong bias toward momentum — iterating in public, learning from real users, and treating "done" as a competitive advantage.

03
OPEN > CLOSED

The knowledge and tools that got me here came from people who shared freely. I contribute to open source, write in public, and believe that transparency in AI development isn't idealism — it's a safety requirement.

04
IMPACT > METRICS

I optimize for actual human value created, not numbers that look good on a dashboard. The most important question about any system I build is: does this make someone's life meaningfully better?

05
CURIOUS > CERTAIN

The smartest move in a fast-moving field is staying genuinely uncertain and curious. I read outside my domain constantly, change my mind when evidence demands it, and distrust anyone — including myself — who's too confident.

06
HUMANS > SYSTEMS

Technology serves people, not the other way around. I hold a strong ethical line around AI that manipulates, deceives, or disempowers. Building AI that augments human agency is the only work worth doing.
